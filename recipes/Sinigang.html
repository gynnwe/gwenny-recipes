Sinigang na Baboy

What could possibly be disliked about pork sinigang or the dish in general? I grew up loving all of its iterations and variations. People continue to prepare this popular Filipino dish because of some component in it. Personally, I enjoy the way the hot, sour broth makes me feel. I feel warm and cozy because of it while it's cold outside. On hot summer days, it still has the same calming effect on me, but for some reason, the warm soup almost literally makes me feel chilly.

My mother was not fond of cooking Sinigang when I was a child. Although preparing this meal is a once-in-a-blue-moon occurrence, the pork sinigang's sour flavor made me gain interest in making it. As the go-to girl who purchases ingredients every time my mom cooks, I once tried purchasing Pork Sinigang ingredients also and cooked the meal on my own. Turns out, it was good! My Filipino heart feels fulfilled.

This is a recipe for pork belly soup cooked in sour broth. It makes use of a variety of vegetables and is best when served hot with a cup of rice on the side.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly sliced into cubes
  • 2 to 3 cups kangkong leaves
  • 3 pieces long green chili pepper siling pansigang
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 1 cup sliced daikon radish labano
  • 10 to 12 pieces snake beans cut in 2 inch length pieces
  • 1 piece onion quartered
  • 15 pieces okra
  • 2 pieces taro peeled and cut in half (gabi)
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 packs Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix Original (20 gram pack)
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce patis
  • Â¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Pour water into a cooking pot. Let boil
  2. Add onion and tomato. Boil for 5 to 8 minutes.
  3. Put the pork in the cooking pot. Boil for 25 minutes.
  4. Add the taro. Cover and continue to boil for 30 minutes.
  5. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.
  6. Put the daikon radish in. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Add the chili peppers, okra, and snake beans. Cook for 7 minutes.
  8. Add the kangkong. Cover the cooking pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to as serving bowl. Serve with a small bowl with patis and spicy chili.
  10. Share and enjoy!

Notes

  • Use any cut of pork that you prefer. Beef can also be used for this recipe.
  • If using it, add more cooking time to ensure that the beef gets tender.
